Chapter 01

Built for precision
from the start.

Engineering came first.

Years working across defence and aerospace — industries where zero margin for error is the baseline you work from. Components built to function in all environments, under all conditions. Mechanical design. Electrical systems. Without exception, every time.

Chapter 02

A deliberate
departure.

At 30, the decision was made to step away from engineering entirely and pursue photography professionally. What followed were years moving between Thailand, Athens, and Milan — shooting across some of the most demanding creative environments in Europe.

The engineering mind didn't switch off. It just started observing something new.

Chapter 03

The same frustration.
Every single set.

Anyone who has worked professionally on set knows the feeling. It doesn't announce itself. It accumulates — quietly, persistently — until it becomes something you've simply learned to work around.

What it took to address wasn't obvious. Mechanical design. Electrical integration. Software built from nothing. Algorithms conceived, tested, discarded, rebuilt. The answer doesn't exist in any single discipline — it lives in the space where several of them meet.

That space took years of training to reach.

Chapter 04

The prototype exists.

Focused development. The kind of obsessive iteration that only makes sense when you've lived on both sides of the problem. The engineering is done. The prototype is built.

What comes next changes the way imaging professionals work.

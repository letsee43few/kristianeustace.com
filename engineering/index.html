About

Engineering
from the inside out.

Kristian Eustace trained as a precision mechanical engineer — years designing and building components where the tolerance for error was zero. Working closely with complex electrical systems, he developed a discipline for how mechanical and electronic elements must operate in complete harmony.

At 30, he walked away from engineering to pursue something equally demanding. What followed was years spent in Thailand, Athens, and Milan — on set, behind the lens, working as a photographer in some of the most competitive creative environments in Europe.

On every set, the same frustration returned. A problem that no product had ever addressed. Not even close. The kind of problem that, once you see it clearly, you cannot unsee — and that took years of focused engineering work to finally solve.

That frustration became Eustace Engineering Systems.

How We Work

Built on three
non-negotiables.

01

Precision

Years of professional engineering taught us that tolerance is not a guideline. It is the difference between something that works and something that fails. Every Eustace Engineering Systems component is held to that standard.

02

Purpose

We don't design for a catalogue. Every component we make exists because a real problem on a real set demanded a better answer.

03

Craft

Engineering and photography share the same discipline: patience, obsession, and an unwillingness to accept good enough. Eustace Engineering Systems brings both to every component — from the first sketch to the finished part.
